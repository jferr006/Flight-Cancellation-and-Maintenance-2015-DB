## Overview
This project presents a comprehensive analysis of aviation operational data using the **Flight Cancellation and Maintenance 2015 DB**. The objective is to evaluate the relationship between scheduled flight operations, mechanical maintenance logs, and systemic disruptions to identify factors impacting airline reliability and efficiency during December 2015.

## Brief Description
This database focuses on the United States domestic aviation sector during December 2015. It provides a structured environment to audit flight histories, focusing on the intersection of **mechanical health** and **operational stressors**. While based on the U.S. DOT Air Travel Consumer Report (originally containing over 5 million flights), this specific dataset was modified using Generative AI for project-specific purposes.By analyzing specific cancellation types—ranging from weather events to carrier-specific maintenance issues—this project identifies performance patterns across various aircraft models and airline carriers to pinpoint bottlenecks in the aviation industry.

## Objectives
* **Analyze the correlation** between maintenance logs and flight cancellations.
* **Evaluate airline performance** 
* **Support data-driven strategies** for improving ticket prices and airline model recommendations.

## Key Analysis & Queries
The project includes multiple analytical queries covering:
* **Carrier Reliability Ranking:** Assessing which airlines handled December stressors most effectively.
* **Maintenance Impact Analysis:** Measuring how mechanical logs translate to aircraft reliability.
* **Cancellation Type Distribution:** Categorizing disruptions and their frequencies.
* **Time Series Analysis:** Popular Days and their corresponding cancellation rates.

---
*Note: This dataset specifically covers United States domestic flights from December 1, 2015, to December 31, 2015.*
